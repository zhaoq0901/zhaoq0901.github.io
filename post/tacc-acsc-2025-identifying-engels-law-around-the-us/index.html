TACC ACSC 2025: Identifying Engel's Law around the U.S.

2025-07-24 4 min read

What is Engel's Law?

Engel's Law: As household's income , the % of income spent on food will decrease, although the amount of food expenditure may increase.

This means when people solve their basic living cost, they will have more spending on other goods and services, such as education and recreation.

Engel's Coefficient = Food Expenditure / Household Income

  • Close to 1:
    • The family spent most of their income on food
  • Close to 0:
    • The family spent less of their income on food

Why should we care?

By calculating the Engel's coefficient, we can know the familiy's living standard. If their coefficient is high, that means they are struggling with food accessibility. Therefore, they lack money for other services, such as education and healthcare.

Research Question

What is the relationship between Household Income and Food Expenditure in the U.S. from 2015 to 2019?

Why Household Income?

  • Since indiviude may have different eating habits, such as vegetarian or anorexia, those will the factor to affect the result.

What is Food Expenditure?

  • The amount of money spent on food (Food-at-home & Food-away-from-home)
    • Food-at-home (FAH):
      • The food and beverages purchased from grocery stores, supermarkets, convenience stores, and other retail outlets for consumption at home.
    • Food-away-from-home (FAFH):
      • All meals and snacks purchased from restaurants, cafes, fast-food outlets, and other establishments where food is prepared and consumed outside the home.

Why 2015 to 2019?

  • I want to do the researching in 5 years period. However, due to the pendamic happend, the 2020 data of household income is missing, and the food expenditure data weren't update after 2023.

Methodology

  • Dataset:
    • US Census: Household Income American Community Survey (ACS) 1-year estimate
    • USDA: Food Expenditure Series
    • USDA: Current Population Survey (CPS) Food Security Supplement
  • System and tool:
    • Stampede3 provide by Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC)
    • Python3 with Jupyter Notebook

However, there is an outlier: California.

Consistently from 2015 to 2019: although people from California can make a good amount of money, there are still a lot of families that are struggling with food security.

Food Security In CA

⬆Definition of Food Security

The documentation of the Current Population Survey (CPS) states that the data represents 20% of the California population.

Therefore, 20% of California's population experiences food insecurity.

Among these families, 90% of them are receiving food from the food distribution program or are on the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP).

The rest 10% are hard to access nutritious food, and even skip meals every day.

Conclusion

The data analysis mostly supports Engel's Law.

  • Higher household income → lower % of income spent on food
  • Familes with higher household income can inveset their money on other supplement goods or services beyong the basic needs.

However, under certain circumstances (such as CA), Engel's Law may not hold true. The potential reason behind this could be:

  • Food prices
  • Population density
  • Distance from grocery stores
