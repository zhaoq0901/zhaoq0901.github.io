ACSC 2025: Identifying Engel's Law around the U.S.

2026-03-14 6 min read

This reseach has been revised for presenting in 2026 Emerging Researchers National (ERN) Conference.

What is Engel's Law?

Engel's Law: As household's income , the % of income spent on food will decrease, although the amount of food expenditure may increase.

This means when people solve their basic living cost, they will have more spending on other goods and services, such as education and recreation.

Engel's Coefficient = Food Expenditure / Household Income

  • Close to 1:
    • The family spent most of their income on food
  • Close to 0:
    • The family spent less of their income on food

Why should we care?

By calculating the Engel's coefficient, we can know the familiy's living standard. If their coefficient is high, that means they are struggling with food accessibility. Therefore, they lack money for other services, such as education and healthcare.

Research Question

What is the relationship between Household Income and Food Expenditure in the U.S. from 2021 to 2024?

Why Median Household Income?

  • It represents the "typical" household's earnings by identifying the exact midpoint of all incomes, with 50% above and 50% below. Unlike the average (mean), the median is not distorted by extreme outliers, such as a few extremely high earners, making it a more accurate measure of economic well-being.

What is Food Expenditure?

  • The amount of money spent on food (Food-at-home & Food-away-from-home)
    • Food-at-home (FAH):
      • The food and beverages purchased from grocery stores, supermarkets, convenience stores, and other retail outlets for consumption at home.
    • Food-away-from-home (FAFH):
      • All meals and snacks purchased from restaurants, cafes, fast-food outlets, and other establishments where food is prepared and consumed outside the home.

Why 2021 to 2024?

  • This research was intended to examine people's living standards during the post-COVID-19 period. Because of the pandemic, the 2020 household income data is missing.

Hypothesis

Higher MHI will exhibit lower Engel Coefficients, reflecting a smaller proportion of income allocated to food.

Methodology

  • Calculate the Engel Coefficient for all 50 U.S. states (Engel's Coefficient = Food Expenditure / Median Household Income).
  • Identify the top 10 states with the highest Engel Coefficients; compare the Median Household Income (MHI) of these states against their respective Engel Coefficients to test the validity of Engel's Law.
  • Identify the potential reason, food price, for the outlier.

Data Dictionary:

  • US Census: Household Income American Community Survey (ACS) 1-year estimate
  • USDA: Food Expenditure Series
  • USDA: Current Population Survey (CPS) Food Security Supplement
  • Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis: Per Capita Personal Consumption Expenditures.

Data vistulization & analysis

Engel Coefficient by State (2021 to 2024)

This geographic heatmap indicates families' Engel coefficients by states. The color shifts to yellow where households have an Engel coefficient close to 1, which means they are struggling with basic food needs.

The Engel Coefficient of California (1.08) and Texas (1.04) exceed 1.

That indicate that many families in California and Texas face significant challenges in meeting basic nutritional needs.

Engel Coefficient vs. Household Income (Top 10 States by Engel Coefficient)

Expectation: Engel Coefficient is high because the family has less MHI because they need to put more money toward food.

However, there is an outlier: California.

Among the 10 states with the highest Engel coefficients, while California’s MHI ($93,000) exceeds the U.S. average ($74,000), it maintains the highest Engel Coefficient, highlighting a severe wealth gap that impacts food affordability.

Although people from California can make a good amount of money, many families are struggling with food security.

Price Change for Major Consumer Price Index Categories in CA

From 2021 to 2024, the food service Consumer Price Index (CPI) rose 55.13 percent, the second-highest increase among all services in CA.

According to the USDA report Food Prices and Spending, national all-food CPI rose 23.6% from 2020 to 2024; however, California's food price increases outpaced most other states (55.13%), further straining household budgets despite its high-income status.

Food Security In CA

⬆Definition of Food Security

The documentation of the Current Population Survey (CPS) states that the data represents 20% of the California population.

Therefore, 20% of California's population experiences food insecurity.

Among these families, 90% of them are receiving food from the food distribution program or are on the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP).

The rest 10% are hard to access nutritious food, and even skip meals every day.

Conclusion

The data generally support Engel's Law; however, exceptions such as California suggest the relationship weakens when food prices rise rapidly, particularly from 2021 to 2024.

The data analysis mostly supports Engel's Law.

  • Higher household income → lower % of income spent on food
  • Familes with higher household income can inveset their money on other supplement goods or services beyong the basic needs.

However, under certain circumstances (such as CA), Engel's Law may not hold true. The potential reason behind this could be:

  • Food prices
  • Population density
  • Distance from grocery stores
